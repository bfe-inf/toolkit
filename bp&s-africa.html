Data Management Best Practices & Standards: Africa

The following resources contain data management best practices information for research organizations (both within and outside of the Belmont Forum) in Africa:

  • Organization: African Open Science Platform (Continent-Wide)
    Link: http://africanopenscience.org.za/
    Contains: Links to training, webinars, conferences, and policies on data management and best practices & standards for the entire African continent, as well as country-specific resources for Benin, Botswana, Egypt, Ethiopia, Europe, Ghana, Kenya, Madagascar, South Africa, Tanzania, Togo, and Uganda.
  • Organization: Department of Science and Technology - DST (South Africa)
    Link: South African Research Infrastructure Roadmap
    Contains: A research infrastructure roadmap for South Africa including information on funding requirements and data management.
  • Organization: National Research Foundation - NRF (South Africa)
    Policies: As of March 2015, authors of research papers generated from research either fully or partially funded by NRF, when submitting and publishing in academic journals, should deposit their final peer-reviewed manuscripts that have been accepted by the journals to the administering Institution Repository with an embargo period of no more than 12 months. Earlier Open Access may be provided should this be allowed by the publisher. If the paper is published in an Open Access journal or the publisher allows the deposit of the published version in PDF format, such version should be deposited into the administering Institutional Repository and Open Access should be provided as soon as possible. In addition, the data supporting the publication should be deposited in an accredited Open Access repository, with the provision of a Digital Object Identifier for future citation and referencing. More information can be found at the following link:
    http://www.nrf.ac.za/media-room/news/statement-open-access-research-publications-national-research-foundation-nrf-funded
  • Organization: Strategic Support Programme for Scientific Research - PASRES (Ivory Coast)
    Policies: PASRES does not have a written policy on data sharing and does not outline a data management plan.
